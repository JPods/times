? Route-Time — User Guide
What It Is

Route-Time designs and simulates JPods networks on a real map. Place stations and traffic circles, connect them, then run a simulation to get door-to-door travel times for every station pair.

Build a Network — Step by Step
  1. Choose a station orientation (N–S, E–W, NE–SW, NW–SE) in the Stations panel, then click the map to place it. The platform side loads passengers; the far side reverses direction.
  2. Place traffic circles at intersections (⬡ Circle or ⬡ /45°). Circles connect up to four guideway arms.
  3. Connect structures: click a blue outbound CP dot, then click the red inbound CP dot on the adjacent structure. Or click ⚡ Auto-Connect to wire everything automatically.
  4. Click ▶ Run to simulate. Results appear in the sidebar; the animation starts automatically.
Connection Points (CPs)
Blue dot Outbound — vehicles depart this end
Red dot Inbound — vehicles arrive this end
Shift+click CP Disconnect a connected pair

Always connect blue → red. Each connection creates one directed guideway segment. Two segments (one each direction) form a complete two-way corridor.

Station Layout
Near side Platform — passengers board and alight here
Far side No platform — internal U-turn reverses direction
CP_near_far Exit toward near-guideway direction
CP_far_near Exit toward far-guideway direction (via internal U-turn)
Animation — Pod Colors
White — parked at platform (loading or unloading)
Orange — in station siding (entering or leaving platform)
Yellow — approaching destination station
Red→Green — in transit (red = slow ends, green = cruise speed)

Use 5×–20× playback speed to follow individual pods. 360×+ shows network-level flow. All station pairs are simulated simultaneously.

Simulation Results
  • The sweep dispatches one pod from every station to every other station.
  • Travel times include station overhead (ticketing, boarding, alighting) — default 150 sec total.
  • The travel time matrix shows median door-to-door minutes for each pair.
  • ⊙ Isochrone — click any map point to see walk-ride-walk coverage zones (5/10/20/30 min).
